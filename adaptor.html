The two phases

Different phases.
One continuous record.

The Adaptor doesn't ask you to do an internship in isolation. It builds one on top of something you've already created — so everything is connected and verifiable from day one.

01
🏗️

Build Your Project

You start by building a real project in a domain you choose. Any domain — software, data, hardware, ML, security, anything. You own every decision: the scope, the architecture, the tech.

Phase 02 · Weeks 5–12

Intern Mode

Once your project is verified, you shift into intern mode. Your mentor now assigns you tasks inside your own project — adding features, fixing issues, improving documentation, extending functionality.

→
Tasks are assigned weekly with clear scope and acceptance criteria
→
You work within constraints you didn't set — the way real junior roles work
→
Every completed task is logged to your verified profile in real time
→
Mentor reviews and signs off each sprint before the next begins
What intern mode looks like

Structured work.
Not busywork.

Intern mode is designed to simulate how real tasks reach junior engineers — defined scope, real deadlines, someone reviewing your output. Here's what it actually involves.

Who it's for

Built for students who want
more than just a project.

The Adaptor is for students who know a solo project isn't quite enough — and want structured internship experience on top of it, without waiting for a company to give them a chance.

🎯

You're applying for your first job or internship soon

You need something stronger than a GitHub link. The Adaptor gives you a verified project plus a documented internship task record — both shareable in the same profile URL.

🔄

You want to know how real team work actually feels

Working on tasks someone else assigns — within a scope you didn't set — is fundamentally different from building your own project. This gives you that experience before day one at a company.

📈

You want internship experience without depending on one

You've applied for internships and been rejected for not having experience. The Adaptor breaks that loop by giving you structured, mentor-supervised, verified work experience you can point to.
